## 📦 What Is This?

This is a **complete, plain-English education bundle** for understanding, writing, and mastering software licenses — from MIT and GPL to EULAs, Privacy Policies, and custom Source Available licenses.

Three documents. 35+ pages. Zero legal jargon left unexplained.

Most license resources are written by lawyers for lawyers. This bundle was written for **developers, indie hackers, students, and first-time founders** who just want to understand what they're agreeing to — and how to write their own.

## 📘 Document 1 — License School
### *`LICENSE_SCHOOL.md` — 10 Pages*

#### What It Covers
The complete beginner's guide to understanding every major software license in existence. No experience required. No law degree required.

| Section | What You Learn |
|---|---|
| What Is a License? | The "rules of your toy" analogy — zero assumptions |
| The Three Worlds | Open Source vs. Source Available vs. Proprietary explained simply |
| Every OSS License | MIT, Apache 2.0, GPL v2/v3, LGPL, AGPL, MPL, BSD, ISC — all of them |
| Source Available | BSL, SSPL, Fair Source, Commons Clause — the in-between zone |
| Proprietary & EULA | What you're actually agreeing to when you click "I Agree" |
| Privacy Policies | Why they're required by law and what must be in them |
| Compatibility Charts | Which licenses can work together and which can't |
| How to Apply a License | Step-by-step for a real project |
| Rookie Mistakes | The 8 mistakes that trip up real developers |
| Master Cheat Sheet | One-page reference card for everything |

#### Why It's Better Than Most Others

Most license guides either:
- List licenses with no context (*"MIT is permissive. Apache adds patent protection."* — okay, but so what?)
- Are written for people who already understand copyright law
- Stop at open source and ignore EULAs and privacy policies entirely
- Never explain WHY licenses have the terms they do

**This document explains the philosophy behind every license.** You don't just learn what GPL says — you learn WHY Richard Stallman wrote it, what problem he was solving, and why that problem matters to you today. You don't just see a compatibility table — you see real scenarios with real consequences.

It also covers the full picture: OSS, source available, proprietary, EULA, and privacy policy in one place. Most resources only cover one category.

## 📗 Document 2 — License Maker School
### *`LICENSE_MAKER_SCHOOL.md` — 20 Pages*

#### What It Covers
A step-by-step course in actually writing your own licenses — from scratch — for every major license type. Includes full templates, real history, vocabulary, and activities.

| Section | What You Learn |
|---|---|
| How Lawyers Think | The 5 questions every lawyer answers before writing anything |
| History of License Writing | Who invented each license, why, and what problem they were solving |
| Anatomy of a License | The 8 body parts every license shares |
| Legal Language Decoded | 30+ legal terms translated into plain English |
| Writing an OSS License | Step-by-step sentence-by-sentence construction |
| Writing a EULA | Full template with every required section |
| Writing a Privacy Policy | GDPR + CCPA compliant full template |
| Writing a Source Available License | The business-first template for startups |
| Blank Templates | All four document types ready to copy and fill in |
| 7 Activities | Beginner to advanced exercises with real scenarios |
| The Final Boss Challenge | Build a complete license suite for a fictional AI startup |
| Code of Ethics | The 7 rules of being a license writer who doesn't make the world worse |

#### Why It's Better Than Most Others

There is almost no resource on the internet that teaches you how to **write** a license from scratch — most resources only explain licenses that already exist. The few that do either:
- Require you to already understand contract law
- Only cover one type (usually just OSS or just EULA)
- Give you a template with no explanation of what each section does or why it's there

**This document teaches you the mental model first.** You learn how lawyers think before you see a single legal clause. You understand WHY a disclaimer must be in ALL CAPS before you write one. You know the history of who wrote GPL and why before you decide whether to use it.

The 7 activities scale from dead-simple (write a lemonade recipe license) to advanced (build a full source available license for a startup protecting itself from cloud giants). The Final Boss challenge — building four complete documents for a fictional AI company — is the kind of capstone exercise most paid law courses skip.

## 📙 Document 3 — License Vocab & Activity Workbook
### *`LICENSE_VOCAB_WORKBOOK.md` — 5 Pages*

#### What It Covers
The companion practice workbook. Twenty essential legal words, the exact phrases to use them in, and a complete guide to fixing every common beginner mistake.

| Section | What You Learn |
|---|---|
| Page 1 — Grant Words | grant, perpetual, irrevocable, non-exclusive, sublicense |
| Page 2 — Restriction Words | derivative work, reverse engineer, competing use, notwithstanding, indemnify |
| Page 3 — Disclaimer Words | warranty, merchantability, limitation of liability, tort, consequential damages |
| Page 4 — Boilerplate Words | governing law, severability, entire agreement, arbitration, amendment |
| Page 5 — Mistake Fix Guide | 10 real mistakes with exact fixes and rewrites |
| Quick Reference Card | All 20 words in a single table — definition, phrase, location |

#### Why It's Better Than Most Others

Legal glossaries exist everywhere. What they almost never include:
- **When** to use each term (and when NOT to)
- **The exact phrase** you copy into a real document
- **The beginner mistake** associated with each term and how to fix it
- **Activities** that make you use the word rather than just read it

This workbook is structured the way vocabulary is actually learned — definition, context, usage, practice. Each page has an activity that forces you to write, not just read. The Mistake Fix Guide on Page 5 is a standalone reference you can return to every time you're reviewing a license you wrote.

The 20-word quick reference card at the end is the kind of thing you print out and pin above your monitor.
